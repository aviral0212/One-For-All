# First 10 Customer Conversations Checklist

Use this checklist before and during your first 10 customer conversations.

## Before the conversation

- [ ] I know who I am trying to talk to
- [ ] I understand the problem I want to explore
- [ ] I have 5 to 7 good questions prepared
- [ ] I am not planning to pitch too early
- [ ] I am ready to listen more than I speak

## Questions to cover

- [ ] What are you currently trying to achieve?
- [ ] What is the hardest part of that right now?
- [ ] How are you solving it today?
- [ ] What do you dislike about current solutions?
- [ ] How often does this problem happen?
- [ ] How important is it to solve?
- [ ] Have you spent money or time trying to fix it?

## During the conversation

- [ ] Let the person speak freely
- [ ] Ask follow-up questions
- [ ] Look for specific examples
- [ ] Notice emotional signals and frustration
- [ ] Do not lead the answers
- [ ] Do not defend your idea

## After the conversation

- [ ] Write down the strongest pain points
- [ ] Note exact phrases they used
- [ ] Record what they already do today
- [ ] Identify whether the problem is urgent or mild
- [ ] Decide whether this supports or weakens the idea

## Warning signs

- [ ] People are only being polite
- [ ] The problem is vague
- [ ] Nobody has taken action on it before
- [ ] Nobody cares enough to change behavior
- [ ] You are hearing praise, not pain

## Success signs

- [ ] People describe the problem clearly
- [ ] They already try to solve it
- [ ] They want something better
- [ ] They ask for follow-up
- [ ] They show strong intent, not casual interest
