# Founder Learning Resources

A practical starter list for early-stage founders and startup builders.

This is not meant to be a giant list of random links. The goal is to keep only resources that are genuinely useful when you are trying to think clearly, validate ideas, and execute better.

## 1. Startup thinking

### Paul Graham Essays
Useful for understanding startup thinking, founder psychology, ambition, focus, and how early-stage companies actually work.

### Y Combinator Library
A strong starting point for validation, product, fundraising, growth, and founder basics. Practical and relevant for first-time builders.

### First Round Review
Useful for operator-level articles on hiring, product, communication, and execution.

## 2. Validation and customer understanding

### The Mom Test
One of the most useful resources for learning how to talk to potential users without collecting fake validation.

### Lean Startup concepts
Helpful for thinking in terms of testing, learning, iteration, and evidence instead of assumptions.

### Customer interview frameworks
Useful when trying to understand pain points, current behavior, and willingness to change.

## 3. Execution and decision-making

### Weekly review systems
Useful for founders who have ideas but struggle with consistency and follow-through.

### Decision-making frameworks
Helpful when choosing between ideas, priorities, and next steps under uncertainty.

### Simple checklists
Good for reducing chaos and making progress more repeatable.

## 4. Communication

### Clear writing
Founders need to write clearly: messages, landing pages, outreach, docs, and internal thinking all improve when writing improves.

### Simple pitching
Being able to explain a problem, user, and solution clearly is a core founder skill.

### Customer outreach basics
Useful for getting early conversations, early feedback, and first users.

## Notes

This file is intentionally curated, not exhaustive.

I want this repository to be useful for early-stage builders, not impressive-looking for the sake of it.
